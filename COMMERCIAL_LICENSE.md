A commercial license is available for use cases that are incompatible with the AGPL or that involve white-label use of the Agience brand.

## Two tracks — when you need a commercial license

### Track 1 — Copyleft opt-out (copyright)

The AGPL-3.0 requires that modified versions and network-accessible deployments make their complete source code available under the same license. A commercial license removes that obligation.

**You need a commercial license if any of the following apply:**

- You modify Agience and want to keep those modifications proprietary (closed-source)
- You offer Agience as a managed or hosted service to third parties without releasing your source code
- You embed or OEM Agience inside a product and do not wish to release that product's source under the AGPL
- You want enterprise support, SLA, compliance, or custom deployment rights

### Track 2 — Trademark (white-label)

The AGPL licenses copyright only — it does **not** grant rights to the "Agience" name, logo, or trade dress. White-label use always requires a separate commercial trademark license, **regardless of whether you comply with the AGPL**.

**You need a commercial trademark license if:**

- You replace Agience product name, logos, or visible identity with your own brand
- You ship desktop shell, browser extension, or similar companion surfaces as your branded product
- You suppress meaningful Agience identity while offering the product to your users

This applies even if you open-source your modifications under the AGPL. AGPL compliance covers the code; it does not grant the right to rebrand.

## What is free

AGPL-compliant, non-white-label use is **free for everyone** — individuals, startups, nonprofits, educational institutions, research labs, and companies of any size. As long as you:

1. Share your source code for modifications and network-accessible deployments under the AGPL, and
2. Do not white-label the product (keep Agience identity visible or use "Powered by Agience")

## What is not white-labeling

- Running Agience on your own domain
- Presenting the product as Agience or "Powered by Agience"
- Internal use with your own SSO, policies, or infrastructure
- Configuration, theming, or deployment customization that does not replace product identity
- Operating MCP servers, sidecars, or supporting infrastructure

If users experience the product as your branded product instead of Agience, treat that as white-labeling.

## Commercial agreement shapes

Commercial deals differ by use case:

- **Self-host** — proprietary modifications, internal enterprise deployment without AGPL source release
- **White-label** — branding rights (trademark license) plus optional copyleft opt-out
- **OEM / embedded** — distribution rights for embedding Agience inside another product
- **Hosted service** — operating Agience as a managed service for third parties

Each shape has different rights, support, branding, and commercial terms.
